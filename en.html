THE TALENTED KUSMANOVA SISTERS

Kikenova (Kusmanova) Aliya Kazkenovna and Bekzhanova (Kusmanova) Kuralai Kazkenovna are natives of the village of Grachi, Beskaragai district, Abai region. The talented sisters Aliya and Kuralai, despite their busy schedules and frequent tours, always strive to participate in the cultural life of their home district. They have taken part in gala concerts such as “Tugan Zher – Altyn Besigim” held during the Beskaragai District Culture Days in the city of Ust-Kamenogorsk.

ALIYA KAZKENOVNA KIKENOVA

A renowned opera singer. In 1989, she graduated from the M. Tulebayev Music College majoring in "Solo Singing". She later completed her education at the Rimsky-Korsakov Saint Petersburg State Conservatory. For many years she has lived in Saint Petersburg and is a leading opera soloist of the local theater. Aliya performs solo concerts in Moscow, Saint Petersburg, and other cities across Russia, as well as in Kazakhstan, Ukraine, Italy, and Switzerland. For over 20 years, Aliya Kikenova has served as a cultural ambassador of Kazakhstan in Saint Petersburg.

KURALAI KAZKENOVNA BEKZHANOVA

A star of Kazakh pop music and a well-known singer. Kuralai gained recognition through her classical vocal performances and became widely loved through her work in pop music. After finishing school, she studied at the Mukhan Tulebayev Music College in Semey, continued at the Kurmangazy Conservatory in Almaty, and completed her postgraduate studies in Astana. She currently resides in Almaty and works at the T. Zhurgenov National Academy of Arts. Together with her husband, Miras, Kuralai performs classical music, arias, romances, and folk songs. Their repertoire also includes modern works by Kazakhstani composers. As the musical duo “Miras & Kuralai Bekzhanov”, they are known as a family ensemble in the national music scene. They are the founders of the “Miras-Kuralai” art studio in Almaty, which provides opportunities for creative growth to gifted young performers. Their repertoire includes songs such as “Asyl Zharim”, “Tugan Zherim – Altyn Besigim”, “Gazhaip Zhandar”, “Moldir Sezim” and many others.
